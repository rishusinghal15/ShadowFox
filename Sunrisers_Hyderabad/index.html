The Story

Orange Army,
One Heart

Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH) also known as The Orange Army entered the Indian Premier League in 2013 as the successor to Deccan Chargers, owned by Sun TV Network. From day one, they brought a brand of cricket built on disciplined bowling and explosive batting.

Under David Warner's leadership, SRH conquered the IPL in 2016, and the franchise has since transformed into one of the most feared batting units in T20 history, breaking the all-time IPL scoring record with 287/3 against RCB in April 2024.

The Orange Army plays out of the Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium, Hyderabad - a fortress where the crowd turns the stands into a blazing sea of orange on every match day.

All Time Greats

SRH Legends

1

Leading Run-Scorer · Batsman

David Warner

🇦🇺 Australia · Captain 2015–2021

The face of SRH. Warner is the franchise's all-time leading run-scorer, topping 500 runs in six of his seven SRH seasons. Won the IPL under his captaincy in 2016 and claimed the Orange Cap three times (2015, 2017, 2019).

3,849 runs for SRH
2

Leading Wicket-Taker · Bowler

Bhuvneshwar Kumar

🇮🇳 India

The ace seamer who defined SRH's bowling identity for nearly a decade. Won the Purple Cap in back-to-back seasons (2016 & 2017). His swing bowling was the cornerstone of the 2016 title win.

170+ wickets for SRH
3

Spin Wizard · Bowler

Rashid Khan

🇦🇫 Afghanistan · 2017–2021

The most economical spinner in IPL history during his SRH tenure. His goggle and leg-break combination made him the MVP for five straight seasons. Batters across the world couldn't read him.

93 wickets in 76 games
4

Gentleman Captain · Batsman

Kane Williamson

🇳🇿 New Zealand · Captain 2018 & 2022

Won the Orange Cap in 2018 with 735 runs and led SRH to their second IPL final. His calm, cerebral captaincy and elegant strokeplay made him one of the most respected players in franchise history.

1,233 runs for SRH

Journey Through Time

Trophy & Milestones

2013
Playoff · 4th

SRH's debut season. Replaced Deccan Chargers. Under Sangakkara & Cameron White, finished 4th and reached the Eliminator — a strong debut for a new franchise.

🏟
📅
2014
Eliminated · 6th

Darren Sammy led SRH through a transitional season. David Warner started emerging as a key batter while the team searched for the right combination.

2015
Orange Cap 🟠

David Warner won his first Orange Cap with 562 runs and was appointed captain, laying the groundwork for the dynasty to come.

🟠
🏆
2016
🏆 IPL Champions

The greatest season. SRH defeated RCB by 8 runs in the final. Warner's batting and Bhuvneshwar's bowling were unstoppable. The Orange Army had its first title!

2017
Qualifier 2

Defending champions nearly retained the title. Warner won another Orange Cap (641 runs), Bhuvneshwar claimed a second consecutive Purple Cap with 26 wickets.

🏅
🥈
2018
🥈 Runners-up

Under Williamson's masterful leadership, SRH reached their second final. Rashid Khan was sensational. Lost to CSK in an epic final at Wankhede.

2019
Orange Cap 🟠

David Warner's third Orange Cap — 692 runs in 12 matches. One of the greatest individual IPL batting campaigns ever recorded.

🟠
📍
2020
Eliminator · 3rd

Played in UAE due to COVID-19. Warner scored 548 runs, T Natarajan's yorker mastery became the story of the season. SRH reached playoffs for the 5th straight year.

2021
Eliminated · 8th

A difficult season — SRH finished 8th and David Warner was controversially dropped mid-season. The end of an era for the Orange Army.

📉
🔄
2022
Eliminated · 8th

Post-mega-auction rebuild. Kane Williamson led the team. Umran Malik emerged as a pace sensation, but the overall campaign fell short.

2023
Last Place · 10th

SRH's worst-ever season — finished bottom. However, Heinrich Klaasen showed his potential. Daniel Vettori was appointed as the new head coach for 2024.

⬇
📈
2024
🥈 Runners-up

A dramatic resurgence. Set the highest-ever IPL total (287/3 vs RCB). Travis Head and Pat Cummins led them to the final, where they fell to KKR by 8 wickets.

2025
Eliminated · 7th

Mohammed Shami returned but was traded mid-season to LSG. SRH finished 7th. Brydon Carse and Harsh Dubey were key finds. Mega rebuild ahead of 2026.

Pride & Glory

Key Achievements

🏆

IPL Champions 2016

Defeated Royal Challengers Bangalore by 8 runs in the final. The Orange Army's finest hour — their maiden IPL title under David Warner's leadership.

📊

IPL Record Score

287/3 vs RCB on April 15, 2024 — the highest-ever team total in IPL history. Travis Head's 89 off just 23 balls was the centrepiece of a historic knock.

🥈

Three Finals

SRH have appeared in three IPL finals (2016, 2018, 2024), winning the title in 2016. Their 2024 campaign was arguably their most dominant batting display ever.

🎖

4 Orange Caps

David Warner won 3 Orange Caps (2015, 2017, 2019) and Kane Williamson won 1 (2018) — the most Orange Caps won by players from a single franchise.

🎳

2 Purple Caps

Bhuvneshwar Kumar's back-to-back Purple Caps in 2016 (23 wickets) and 2017 (26 wickets) are a testament to his consistency and swing-bowling artistry.

🔥

5 Straight Playoffs

From 2016 to 2020, SRH qualified for the playoffs in every single season — one of the longest consistent playoff streaks in IPL franchise history.
