The Story

Orange Army,
One Heart

Sunrisers Hyderabad (SRH) entered the Indian Premier League in 2013 as the successor to Deccan Chargers, owned by Sun TV Network. From day one, they brought a brand of cricket built on disciplined bowling and explosive batting.

Under David Warner's leadership, SRH conquered the IPL in 2016, and the franchise has since transformed into one of the most feared batting units in T20 history — breaking the all-time IPL scoring record with 287/3 against RCB in April 2024.

The Orange Army plays out of the Rajiv Gandhi International Cricket Stadium, Hyderabad — a fortress where the crowd turns the stands into a blazing sea of orange on every match day.

All Time Greats

SRH Legends

1

Leading Run-Scorer · Batsman

David Warner

🇦🇺 Australia · Captain 2015–2021

The face of SRH. Warner is the franchise's all-time leading run-scorer, topping 500 runs in six of his seven SRH seasons. Won the IPL under his captaincy in 2016 and claimed the Orange Cap three times (2015, 2017, 2019).

3,849 runs for SRH
2

Leading Wicket-Taker · Bowler

Bhuvneshwar Kumar

🇮🇳 India

The ace seamer who defined SRH's bowling identity for nearly a decade. Won the Purple Cap in back-to-back seasons (2016 & 2017). His swing bowling was the cornerstone of the 2016 title win.

170+ wickets for SRH
3

Spin Wizard · Bowler

Rashid Khan

🇦🇫 Afghanistan · 2017–2021

The most economical spinner in IPL history during his SRH tenure. Batters across the world couldn't read him. His goggle and leg-break combination made him the MVP for five straight seasons.

93 wickets in 76 games
4

Gentleman Captain · Batsman

Kane Williamson

🇳🇿 New Zealand · Captain 2018 & 2022

Won the Orange Cap in 2018 with 735 runs and led SRH to their second IPL final appearance. His calm, cerebral captaincy and elegant strokeplay made him one of the most respected players in franchise history.

1,233 runs for SRH
5

The Lynchpin · Bowler

Dale Steyn

🇿🇦 South Africa · 2013–2014

The pace legend who set the tone for SRH's bowling culture in their inaugural 2013 season. His 19 wickets at an economy of 5.66 were instrumental in SRH winning 9 games and reaching the playoffs in their debut year.

28 wickets in 24 games
6

The Death Bowler · Bowler

T Natarajan

🇮🇳 India

From a small town in Tamil Nadu to India's national team — Natarajan's yorker mastery made him a breakout star during the 2020 IPL. His ability to nail death-over deliveries under pressure made him SRH's secret weapon.

16 wickets in IPL 2020

Trophy & Milestones

Playoff · 4th

SRH's debut season. Replaced Deccan Chargers. Under Sangakkara and Cameron White, finished 4th and reached the Eliminator — a strong debut for a new franchise.

David Warner won his first Orange Cap with 562 runs. Warner was appointed captain, laying the groundwork for the dynasty to come.

Pride & Glory

Key Achievements

🏆

IPL Champions 2016

Defeated Royal Challengers Bangalore by 8 runs in the final. The Orange Army's finest hour — their maiden IPL title under David Warner's leadership.

📊

IPL Record Score

287/3 vs RCB on April 15, 2024 — the highest-ever team total in IPL history. Travis Head's 89 off just 23 balls was the centrepiece of a historic knock.

🥈

Three Finals

SRH have appeared in three IPL finals (2016, 2018, 2024), winning the title in 2016. Their 2024 campaign was arguably their most dominant batting display ever.

🎖

4 Orange Caps

David Warner won 3 Orange Caps (2015, 2017, 2019) and Kane Williamson won 1 (2018) — the most Orange Caps won by players from a single franchise.

🎳

2 Purple Caps

Bhuvneshwar Kumar's back-to-back Purple Caps in 2016 (23 wickets) and 2017 (26 wickets) are a testament to his consistency and swing-bowling artistry.

🔥

5 Straight Playoffs

From 2016 to 2020, SRH qualified for the playoffs in every single season — one of the longest consistent playoff streaks in IPL franchise history.
